From Prediction to Activation Discipline

Most execution research conflates directional forecasting with execution gating. GIE-Soliton separates the two by introducing an Admissibility Gate (𝒜). We do not predict the next move; we determine whether the current market structure is structurally eligible for activation based on participation density.

The core shift: from "where will price go?" to "is this displacement structurally admissible?" This eliminates a large class of false positives generated by dense two-sided participation — moves that appear large on the price axis but carry no forward persistence.

Atomic Integrity (AI) — Formal Definition

Let $P_t$ denote the mid-price and $\text{Vol}_t$ the executed volume at time $t$. Over lookback window $W \in \mathbb{Z}_{>0}$:

$$M_t = \sum_{i=t-W}^{t} \text{Vol}_i \qquad \Delta P_t = P_t - P_{t-W}$$

The Atomic Integrity metric at time $t$ is defined as:

$$\text{AI}_t = \frac{M_t}{|\Delta P_t| + \varepsilon}$$
$M_t$ — Executed Mass
Cumulative traded volume over window $W$. Measures total market participation supporting the observed displacement.
$|\Delta P_t|$ — Displacement
Net absolute price movement over $W$. Captures kinematic magnitude without directional bias.
$\varepsilon = 10^{-9}$
Numerical stabiliser preventing division-by-zero. Chosen below any physically meaningful price movement at Binance tick resolution.
Low $\text{AI}_t$
Price moved significantly on thin volume — hallmark of a liquidity void. These events exhibit higher forward persistence $R_{t \to t+H}$ (Ma 2026, Figure 2).
High $\text{AI}_t$
Dense participation underpins the displacement. High reversion probability. Gate closed.

The admissible set (Ma 2026, §4.1):

$$\mathcal{A} = \bigl\{t : \text{AI}_t \leq \tau\bigr\}$$

where $\tau$ is the $p$-th percentile of the trailing empirical distribution of $\text{AI}_t$. Primary specification: $p = 5\%$, $W = H = 100$ ticks, $\varepsilon = 10^{-9}$.

Welford's Algorithm — Non-Asymptotic Numerical Stability

At millisecond resolution over 5,500+ instruments, naive rolling variance via $(\sum x^2) - n\bar{x}^2$ accumulates catastrophic cancellation error. All rolling statistics ($M_t$, $\Phi$, trailing $\tau$ distribution) use Welford's Online Algorithm:

$$\bar{x}_n = \bar{x}_{n-1} + \frac{x_n - \bar{x}_{n-1}}{n} \qquad S_n = S_{n-1} + (x_n - \bar{x}_{n-1})(x_n - \bar{x}_n)$$

$O(1)$ time and $O(1)$ space with zero accumulated floating-point drift. A strict requirement for tick-by-tick deployment where stability cannot be deferred to batch recomputation.

Algorithm AtomicIntegrityGate — deterministic single-pass: Input: tick stream (P_t, V_t), window W, threshold tau State: price_deque(maxlen=W+1), cum_vol=0, ai_history=[] for each tick t: price_deque.append(P_t) cum_vol += V_t if deque full: cum_vol -= V_{t-W} ← O(1) slide delta_P = |price_deque[-1] - price_deque[0]| AI_t = cum_vol / (delta_P + 1e-9) tau = percentile(ai_history, 5) ← trailing dist. if AI_t <= tau: activate downstream ← t ∈ 𝒜

Ψ Operator — Discrete Laplacian & Adaptive Prediction Weight

The second-order price difference $\Psi_t$ (discrete Laplacian) serves as a real-time price acceleration estimator:

$$\Psi_t = P_t - 2P_{t-1} + P_{t-2}$$

This is the discrete analog of $\partial^2 P / \partial t^2$. It is asymmetric under time reversal in the presence of directional flow, giving it causal sensitivity to structural breaks. The prediction weight for kinematic latency compensation is:

$$w = \exp\!\bigl(-k \cdot |\Psi|_{\text{normalized}}\bigr)$$

When $|\Psi_t| \approx 0$ (constant-velocity regime), $w \to 1$ and full kinematic compensation $\hat{P} = P + v\Delta t + \tfrac{1}{2}a\Delta t^2$ is applied. When $|\Psi_t|$ is large (accelerating/structural regime), $w \to 0$ and raw observed price is used — preventing the compensator from amplifying model error during regime breaks.
